Moonlighting

Tech moonlighting refers to the practice of working on a side project or taking on freelance work outside of one's regular job in the tech industry. This can be a common occurrence for tech professionals who are looking to explore new areas of the industry, gain additional experience, or simply make extra money.
There are a variety of reasons why tech professionals might choose to moonlight. For some, it may be a way to stay current with new technologies and keep their skills up to date. Others may be looking to explore new areas of the tech industry, such as mobile app development or cloud computing, that may not be within their current job scope. Some may simply be looking to make extra money to supplement their regular income.
Tech moonlighting can be a great way for tech professionals to gain additional experience and expand their skill set. However, it can also be challenging. Balancing a full-time job with a side project can be difficult, and it can be even more challenging if the side project is in a completely different area of the tech industry. It can also be difficult to find time to work on a side project, especially if the tech professional has a demanding job.
There are a few key things that tech professionals can do to make tech moonlighting more successful. One is to set clear boundaries between their full-time job and their side project. This can involve setting aside specific blocks of time to work on the side project, such as evenings or weekends. It can also involve creating a separate workspace for the side project, to help keep the two separate.
Another key to successful tech moonlighting is to be honest with one's employer about the side project. Many companies have policies in place that allow employees to take on freelance work outside of their regular job, as long as it does not interfere with their regular duties. By being upfront with one's employer about the side project, tech professionals can avoid any potential conflicts of interest or legal issues.
Overall, tech moonlighting can be a great way for tech professionals to gain additional experience and expand their skill set. However, it is important to carefully manage the balance between one's full-time job and side project, and to be honest with one's employer about the side project. By doing so, tech professionals can make tech moonlighting a successful and rewarding experience.
